Question 1: Download 5 datasets (including at least 1 vector and 1 raster) that cover a common region of interest (i.e. the city of Albuquerque, NM, etc.), and copy them to your data directory on the class server. Run ogrinfo or gdalinfo (as appropriate) for each of the datasets you stored on the server. Print out the first 5 lines of the output from gdalinfo or ogrinfo for each of the datasets.

To complete this exercise, I found two raster layers to serve as base maps, ten vector datasets for overlays, and three KML layers, listed below. Note: I have only provided GDAL and OGRINFO for six of them, one more than required for the exam.

List of Data Layers Acquired for This Exercise:

  • A global image showing shaded relief in color (raster dataset: HYP_HR_SR_OB_DR.tif Source: www.naturalearthdata.com)
  • A global image showing shaded relief in grayscale (raster dataset: GRAY_HR_SR.tif Source: www.naturalearthdata.com)
  • Global coastlines (vector/line dataset: ne_10m_coastline Source: www.naturalearthdata.com)
  • Maya structures from the modern town of Blue Creek, Belize (vector/polygon dataset: structures.shp Source: Maya Research Program)
  • Water and wetland polygon data from Belize (vector/polygon dataset: BLZ_water_areas_dcw.shp Source: www.diva-gis.org)
  • Stream data from Belize (vector/line dataset: BLZ_water_line_dcw.shp Source: www.diva-gis.org)
  • Water and wetland polygon data from Guatemala (vector/polygon dataset: GTM_water_areas_dcw.shp Source: www.diva-gis.org)
  • Stream data from Guatemala (vector/line dataset: GTM_water_line_dcw.shp Source: www.diva-gis.org)
  • Water and wetland polygon data from Honduras (vector/polygon dataset: HND_water_areas_dcw.shp Source: www.diva-gis.org)
  • Stream data from Honduras (vector/line dataset: HND_water_line_dcw.shp Source: www.diva-gis.org)
  • Water and wetland polygon data from Mexico (vector/polygon dataset: MEX_water_areas_dcw.shp Source: www.diva-gis.org)
  • Stream data from Mexico (vector/line dataset: MEX_water_line_dcw.shp Source: www.diva-gis.org)
  • A KML showing the biological corridor in the Toledo region, Belize (Source: Biodiversity and Environmental Resource Data System of Belize)
  • A KML showing the biological corridor in the Shipstern-Crooked Tree regions, Belize (Source: Biodiversity and Environmental Resource Data System of Belize)
  • A KML showing the biological corridor in the Maya Mountains, Belize (Source: Biodiversity and Environmental Resource Data System of Belize)
