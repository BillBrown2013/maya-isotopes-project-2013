Geography 585 Final Exam: Map of the Maya World

William T. Brown

Figure 1: The World of the Ancient Maya. The map on the right shows the study area on a larger scale.

This map is different than my previous ones, which deal with the distribution of jade and jadeite minerals in Guatemala. Here, I have combined several attributes of physical geography that act in concert to influence human settlement patterns. For this final map I have taken the maps created in earlier labs and added 10 vector layers (global coastlines, water vector line and polygon data for Mexico, Honduras, Guatemala, and Belize, along with two additional raster layers that serve as alternate base layers for the Blue Marble default base. The vector layers were styled in GeoServer and those styles are shown in my workspace. Also, I added three KML layers showing specific ecosystems in Belize. Notice that almost all of the overlays have been set to invisible; this was done because the map was getting very busy and hard to discern anything. But, I feel that I have left the viewer with a great deal of flexibility when trying to look at the interaction of several variables. A complete list of all layers is provided below. For the previous map, click here. My objective in creating this map is to show geographic variables that may have influenced Maya settlement patterns in prehistory; in order to accomplish my goals, I set a base layer of Blue Marble Next Generation images from EDAC/WMS, an Open Layer base map, along with the newly added layers just described. Hopefully this map will be the ground work for future GIS and spatial analyses that could shed light on Maya settlement patterns and how they changed through time.
